Lights, Camera-AI! CharlestonHacks presents the 30 Second AI Film Fest.

The 30 Second AI Film Fest is a 3-hour AI-generated video hackathon where participants will create innovative, AI-powered 30-second short films. Hosted by CharlestonHacks as part of Dig South and Charleston Tech Week, this event encourages creativity, experimentation, and storytelling through AI-generated content.

30-Second Impact – Our Theme

A great film isn’t just action, it’s a feeling. In just 30 seconds, craft a cinematic moment that captivates, moves, or surprises us and makes us feel something unforgettable. Whether it’s awe, joy, or suspense, every frame must count. Use AI tools, whether for visuals, effects, or storytelling to push creative boundaries and craft a cinematic moment. No wasted time. No empty shots. Just pure impact./p>
Date & Time: May 1st, 6-9 PM
Location: Simmons Center, College of Charleston, Room 180

Rules, Terms and Event Overview

Team Formation: Participants may compete solo or in teams of up to 5 people. Teams can be formed at the event or pre-arranged.

AI Usage & Tools: Films must be created entirely within the 3-hour event window. Teams must use at least one AI-powered tool like OpenArt, our featured AI film sponsor.

Film Requirements: Each film should be 30 seconds long and content must be PG-level. Films must tell a cohesive story or convey a clear concept.

Submission & Presentation: Films must be submitted before the 8:30 PM deadline via the provided submission form. All films will be presented live to the audience and judges.

Judging Criteria: Films will be evaluated based on Creativity & Innovation, Storytelling & Clarity, AI Integration, and Visual & Audio Quality.

Prizes & Recognition: Prizes for Best Film, Most Creative Use of AI, and Audience Favorite. Winners will receive exclusive CharlestonHacks swag, prizes, and recognition.

Terms & Conditions

Eligibility: Open to all attendees of Charleston Tech Week. Participants must be 18+ or have parental consent.

Ownership & Rights: Teams retain full rights to their films but grant CharlestonHacks non-exclusive rights to showcase and promote them for marketing purposes.

Code of Conduct: Be respectful. Collaboration and knowledge sharing are encouraged, but plagiarism is prohibited. Pre-generated work is not allowed.

How to Submit Your Film:

To streamline submissions and showcase all films in one place, we’re asking participants to upload their videos to YouTube on their own accounts and tag them using our official event hashtag.

Upload your 30-second film to your own YouTube channel. Include the event hashtag "#30saiff" in the video title or description. Tag our official YouTube channel (@charlestonHacks-mc4ct) in your description. Once uploaded, we’ll find your film via the hashtag and add it to our official festival playlist!
