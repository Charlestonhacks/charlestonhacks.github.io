harborHack (23) has come to an end, but the journey doesn't stop here.

harborHack (23) was a resounding success, with approximately 100 enthusiastic participants who collectively submitted 14 impressive projects in just one intense weekend. The weekend's events kicked off with an inspiring speech by Patrick Bryant from the Harbor Entrepreneur Center and Code + Trust, setting the tone for the days ahead. Soon after, participants formed teams and then called it a night to recharge for the "grind" day ahead.

From early morning until late into the night, teams tirelessly worked on a diverse range of inspired projects. Their dedication was apparent as they continued to collaborate, even after the last mentor had left the venue. The final day, Sunday, saw teams diligently putting the finishing touches on their presentations before submitting them to our platform. Last-minute adjustments were made, but 14 teams persevered and successfully presented their prototypes. Following the presentations, judges convened in a separate room, while Emma, Gavin, and Amin engaged with the audience. The anticipation grew as the judging panel reached a decision. Lancie announced the winner of the honorable mention award, "Water Watch," Dave declared the second-place team as "StallWart," and Doug proudly revealed the first-place team, "HIVR." We are all immensely proud of the remarkable work achieved during harborHack (23) and eagerly anticipate continuing to support your future endeavors."
